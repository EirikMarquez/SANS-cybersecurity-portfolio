## NCL CTF Experience
Getting to participate on a passiopate team was an entirely new experience. In the past, I have either competed solo, or competed on teams with varying skill levels. However through SANS, I was paired with team mates of a similar skill level, which became immediately evident throughout the competition. 

Key Differences
- Verification: By having multiple team mates, I had people I could count on to ve-verify the answers I discovred, or challenge me in case I missed something. This allowed our team to keep a 98% accuracy for the competition, which became an important factor when the top teams all had a score of 3000.
- Weaknesses: While I am not proficient in password cracking and other fields, by having a team, we were all able to fill each others competenencies, and collaborate to excel within the competition.
- Commradery: Compared to the individual games, my moral for the competition was a lot higher, as I had team mates to talk with, seek help, and overall keep the vibes positive!

Lessons learned:
- AI: AI is un-avoidable in the modern competition scene, however I found it important to utilize AI as a tool rather than a crutch. It became an amazing way to re-verify answers, and to help nudge me in the right direction when I got lost or confused.
- Documentation: When you are sharing answers with a team, you often need to take the time to write how you came up with the answer. This taught me to take notes while I worked through a challenge, rather than waiting till after I solved it, as progressive documentation often came out more natural allowing others to see the process you to took and avoid potential pitfalls.
